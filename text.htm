Robo-t Ltd. (“Robo-t”) operates website Tell.ly. It is Robo-t’s policy to respect your privacy regarding any information we may collect while operating our website.

Website Visitors

Like most website operators, Robo-t collects non-personally-identifying information of the sort that web browsers and servers typically make available, such as the browser type, language preference, referring site, and the date and time of each visitor request. Robo-t’s purpose in collecting non-personally identifying information is to better understand how Robo-t’s visitors use its website. From time to time, Robo-t may release non-personally-identifying information in the aggregate, e.g., by publishing a report on trends in the usage of its website.

Robo-t also collects potentially personally-identifying information like Internet Protocol (IP) addresses for logged in users and for users leaving comments on WordPress.com blogs. Robo-t only discloses logged in user and commenter IP addresses under the same circumstances that it uses and discloses personally-identifying information as described below, except that blog commenter IP addresses are visible and disclosed to the administrators of the blog where the comment was left.

Gathering of Personally-Identifying Information

Certain visitors to Robo-t’s website choose to interact with Robo-t in ways that require Robo-t to gather personally-identifying information. The amount and type of information that Robo-t gathers depends on the nature of the interaction. For example, we ask visitors who sign up at Tell.ly to provide a username and email address. Those who engage in transactions with Robo-t – by purchasing subscription to the Tell.ly telephony service, for example – are asked to provide additional information, including as necessary the personal and financial information required to process those transactions. In each case, Robo-t collects such information only insofar as is necessary or appropriate to fulfill the purpose of the visitor’s interaction with Robo-t. Robo-t does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below. And visitors can always refuse to supply personally-identifying information, with the caveat that it may prevent them from engaging in certain website-related activities.

Aggregated Statistics

Robo-t may collect statistics about the behavior of visitors to its website. Robo-t may display this information publicly or provide it to others. However, Robo-t does not disclose personally-identifying information other than as described below.

Protection of Certain Personally-Identifying Information

Robo-t discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only to those of its employees, contractors and affiliated organizations that (i) need to know that information in order to process it on Robo-t’s behalf or to provide services available at Robo-t’s website, and (ii) that have agreed not to disclose it to others. Some of those employees, contractors and affiliated organizations may be located outside of your home country; by using Robo-t’s website, you consent to the transfer of such information to them. Robo-t will not rent or sell potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information to anyone. Other than to its employees, contractors and affiliated organizations, as described above, Robo-t discloses potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information only in response to a subpoena, court order or other governmental request, or when Robo-t believes in good faith that disclosure is reasonably necessary to protect the property or rights of Robo-t, third parties or the public at large. If you are a registered user of an Robo-t website and have supplied your email address, Robo-t may occasionally send you an email to tell you about new features, solicit your feedback, or just keep you up to date with what’s going on with Robo-t and our products. We primarily use our various product blogs to communicate this type of information, so we expect to keep this type of email to a minimum. If you send us a request (for example via a support email or via one of our feedback mechanisms), we reserve the right to publish it in order to help us clarify or respond to your request or to help us support other users. Robo-t takes all measures reasonably necessary to protect against the unauthorized access, use, alteration or destruction of potentially personally-identifying and personally-identifying information.

Cookies

A cookie is a string of information that a website stores on a visitor’s computer, and that the visitor’s browser provides to the website each time the visitor returns. Robo-t uses cookies to help Robo-t identify and track visitors, their usage of Robo-t website, and their website access preferences. Robo-t visitors who do not wish to have cookies placed on their computers should set their browsers to refuse cookies before using Robo-t’s website, with the drawback that certain features of Robo-t’s website may not function properly without the aid of cookies.

Business Transfers

If Robo-t, or substantially all of its assets were acquired, or in the unlikely event that Robo-t goes out of business or enters bankruptcy, user information would be one of the assets that is transferred or acquired by a third party. You acknowledge that such transfers may occur, and that any acquiror of Robo-t may continue to use your personal information as set forth in this policy.

Privacy Policy Changes

Although most changes are likely to be minor, Robo-t may change its Privacy Policy from time to time, and in Robo-t’s sole discretion. Robo-t encourages visitors to frequently check this page for any changes to its Privacy Policy. Your continued use of this site after any change in this Privacy Policy will constitute your acceptance of such change.
